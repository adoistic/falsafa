## Vision

### 10x Check

The 10x version of "ship the remote MCP for cross-platform consumption" is "ship a complete capability transfer that lets any AI builder, classics scholar, or institutional buyer take Falsafa's methodology and apply it to their own archive in one command, with verifiable evidence the approach works at archive scale, with a path from open-source artifact to peer-reviewed academic citation." That's not 10x bigger in code; it's 10x bigger in *artifact density*. Same backend code; surrounding it: BYOK live demo, eval explorer, vector-DB explainer, pipeline-fork, Perseus showcase, methodology Skill, by-the-numbers stats, academic preprint workflow.

### Platonic Ideal

A reader on HN clicks the Falsafa post. Within the first 60 seconds: they see a live demo (BYOK with their preferred model), they read one paragraph of the writeup, they click into the eval explorer and verify any case they pick, they see the corpus stats with deep links, they read the "why no vector DB" explainer with charts. If they like it: they fork the pipeline and run it on their own archive in one command, they install the gstack Skill into their own coding agent, they cite the preprint in their next paper. The reader feels: *audited, reproducible, complete*. Not marketing — a paper plus a runnable system plus a teachable methodology, all at once.

## Locked Decisions (from this session + prior reviews)

- **Architecture (Approach A):** single remote MCP backend serves all three LLM surfaces (claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini) plus Claude Desktop / Code via stdio adapter where applicable.
- **Launch order:** GPT Store first → claude.ai connector → Gemini Gem.
- **Mode:** SCOPE_EXPANSION (not just the backend; the full launch artifact).
- **Scope (W2):** full Perseus English-translated archive (~1,500 works, ~100M+ words pending W0 verification).
- **Engineering (P6 charter, eng review settled):** SQLite FTS5 for MCP server-side search; LSH/MinHash on TF-IDF for cross-links at scale; Pagefind unchanged for site browser search.
- **Eval (P7):** rigorous adversarial 1,500–3,000 case suite with eval-generation pipeline pulled into PR; Sonnet-judge resolves every cited paragraph_id.
- **Future-fit (P8 softened):** structural compounding properties of markdown + tools, falsifiable via head-to-head hybrid RAG benchmark in the launch artifact itself.
- **Copywriting (no AI tells):** all public-facing copy and the academic preprint avoid AI-writing tells per memory `avoid_ai_writing_tells.md`. Academic prose extension covers conference-paper register.
- **Outreach (show-don't-tell):** no pre-launch pitches to high-signal contacts (Cowen, Karpathy-orbit, EV team). Outreach happens at or after launch with the finished artifact.

## Accepted Scope (now part of the launch artifact)

The launch artifact is no longer "writeup + repo + demo URL." It's:

1. **Writeup** (the launch essay, with concrete numbers, no AI tells)
2. **Repo** (Falsafa monorepo: site, MCP, pipeline, eval suite, Skill, preprint sources)
3. **Live demo URL** at falsafa.ai/try (BYOK, OpenRouter+Anthropic+Gemini, static fallback, deep links)
4. **Eval explorer** at falsafa.ai/eval (browseable adversarial cases vs hybrid RAG)
5. **"Why no vector DB" explainer** at falsafa.ai/thesis (visual, chart-driven)
6. **By-the-numbers** at falsafa.ai/numbers (audit trail with source-data deep links)
7. **Perseus showcase** at falsafa.ai/perseus (reading site landing page)
8. **Pipeline package** published to npm: `npx @falsafa/pipeline ingest <archive>`
9. **gstack Skill** published in gstack's distribution: `gstack skills install falsafa-methodology`
10. **arXiv preprint** with sections compiled from result-milestone Markdown files
11. **PR back to PerseusDL** as part of the open-source-citizen narrative
