Accessibility Statement for Falsafa

Published: 2026-05-14
Conforms to: WCAG 2.2 — Level AA
EN 301 549 v3.2.1 Annex F format.

Compliance status

Falsafa partially conforms to WCAG 2.2 Level AA, with full conformance on selected AAA criteria. "Partially" reflects documented content-type exceptions enumerated below; outside those, every required criterion is supported and verified by automated tests on every pull request.

Non-accessible content

The following content does not meet the higher AAA criteria due to the nature of the published material:

  • WCAG 1.2.1 Audio-only and Video-only (Prerecorded) (A) — Falsafa is a text-only reading platform. No audio or video content is published.
  • WCAG 1.2.2 Captions (Prerecorded) (A) — No video content.
  • WCAG 1.2.3 Audio Description or Media Alternative (Prerecorded) (A) — No video content.
  • WCAG 1.4.2 Audio Control (A) — No auto-playing audio.
  • WCAG 1.2.4 Captions (Live) (AA) — No live media.
  • WCAG 1.2.5 Audio Description (Prerecorded) (AA) — No video content.
  • WCAG 3.3.7 Redundant Entry (AA) — No re-entry forms; site is read-only.
  • WCAG 3.3.8 Accessible Authentication (Minimum) (AA) — No authentication.
  • WCAG 1.2.6 Sign Language (Prerecorded) (AAA) — No video content.
  • WCAG 1.2.7 Extended Audio Description (Prerecorded) (AAA) — No video content.
  • WCAG 1.2.8 Media Alternative (Prerecorded) (AAA) — No video content.
  • WCAG 1.2.9 Audio-only (Live) (AAA) — No live media.
  • WCAG 1.4.7 Low or No Background Audio (AAA) — No audio.
  • WCAG 2.2.5 Re-authenticating (AAA) — Site has no authentication of any kind.
  • WCAG 3.1.3 Unusual Words (AAA) — Falsafa publishes primary-source philosophy across six languages. The corpus is built on technical and unusual vocabulary; identifying every term is contrary to the project's editorial purpose. Partial mitigation planned in V2 via glossary popovers (read_wiki).
  • WCAG 3.1.5 Reading Level (AAA) — Falsafa publishes graduate-level primary-source philosophy across six languages. Lowering reading level would require rewriting the corpus and is contrary to the project's editorial purpose. Mitigations: chapter summaries via read_wiki MCP tool; cross-tradition links via TF-IDF.
  • WCAG 3.1.6 Pronunciation (AAA) — Pronunciation aids for non-English terms across six languages (Urdu, Sanskrit, Old English, French, German, Old Javanese) would require pronunciation curation per term per language — content work explicitly deferred. IPA inline annotations may land in V2 or V3.
  • WCAG 3.3.9 Accessible Authentication (Enhanced) (AAA) — No authentication.

Feedback and contact information

Report any accessibility issue to accessibility@thothica.com or open a GitHub issue at github.com/adoistic/falsafa/issues. We aim to respond within 5 business days.

Enforcement procedure

EU residents may escalate unresolved accessibility complaints to the national enforcement body designated under Directive 2016/2102 in their member state. US residents may file a complaint under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) or Section 508 (for federal accessibility complaints). Indian residents may invoke their rights under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.

Technical specifications

Accessibility of Falsafa relies on the following technologies to work with the particular combination of web browser and any assistive technologies or plugins installed on your computer:

  • HTML
  • WAI-ARIA
  • CSS
  • JavaScript

Assessment approach

Falsafa assessed accessibility through automated CI tests (axe-core, pa11y, Playwright synthetic transcripts) on every pull request, plus real-screen-reader runs (VoiceOver on macOS, NVDA on Windows) via the Guidepup framework. Manual JAWS verification is performed before any major release. All test artifacts (transcripts, audio recordings, screenshots) are committed to the public repository at docs/accessibility/test-runs/.
