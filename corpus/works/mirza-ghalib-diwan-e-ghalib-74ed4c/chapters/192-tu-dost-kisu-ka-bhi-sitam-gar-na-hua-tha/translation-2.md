To all the world, my friend, you were a tyrant star,
But such a common cruelty, my fate had not yet become.

Fate cast me out, a phantom moon, my nascent light to mar;
Before the sun, a worthy rival I had not yet become.

From time's first dawn, our grace is set to meet our spirit's par;
This drop inside my eye, a perfect pearl had not yet become.

Until I saw my love's great presence set the world ajar,
A believer in the Last Day's chaos I had not yet become.

My simple heart is glad her anger raises a new bar;
The lesson of our longing, a repeat had not yet become.

My river of transgression dried, my soul it could not char;
The hem of my own garment, even damp had not yet become.

Asad, my art was drawn from my heart's burning scar;
That fire-temple, for the salamander a fief had not yet become.
