In lonely rooms, I watch the walls so near, I see,
The morning breeze, the messenger I hold so dear, I see.

You came into my house—by God’s own power, it's true,
And now it's you, and now my humble home so near, I see.

May no dark glance fall on your arm, so fine and strong,
So why do they stare at my soul's own bleeding tear, I see?

Why should I look at jewels that sparkle in your hair?
The rising star of gems, their fated worth so clear, I see.
