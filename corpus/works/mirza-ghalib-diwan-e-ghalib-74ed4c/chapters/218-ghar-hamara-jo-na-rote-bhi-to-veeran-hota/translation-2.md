Though I should never weep, my home a waste would be;
If sea were not the sea, a desert waste would be.

Why grieve my heart's tight space? This rebel heart I own,
If not so tightly bound, in wider pain would be.

A life of pious vows should earn a final prize;
If Rizwan kept her gate, my faith not vain would be.
