Let's find a place where no one else is known, and be alone.
No kindred voice, no soul to call my own, and be alone.

A house I'll build of neither wall nor stone,
No neighbor there, no guard, and be alone.

If sickness finds me, let no one hear my moan,
And if I die, no mourner, and be alone.
