Be kind, and call me to you when you please;
I am not bygone time, that I cannot return with ease.

In weakness, why complain of what my rivals say?
It is no physical weight that I cannot lift away.

I search for poison, tormentor, but find no trace;
What is this oath to you, whose hold I cannot erase?

My self-control is gone, I cannot stay apart;
Don't be a pain so great I cannot bear its dart.

If my house burned to ash, what reason for my fear?
It’s not this flame of heart, a fire I cannot clear.

If you won't come, a hundred ways to die are near;
For death is not like you, whom I cannot summon here.

Just smile and call, my grievances will disappear;
What is this thought of you, a ghost I cannot clear?
