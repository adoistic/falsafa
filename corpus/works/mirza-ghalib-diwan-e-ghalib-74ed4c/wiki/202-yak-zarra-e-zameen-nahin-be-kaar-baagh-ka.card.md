## Distinctive trigrams

## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-ce5ce9] Your garden blooms, a board where joy has been designed for the mind,
But the spring cloud—whose wine-cask is it, of what kind of the mind?

## Opens
> [p-95a3a4] No speck of dust is useless, by some grand design of the mind;
The path's a wick that burns within the tulip's scarred design of the mind.

## Closes
> [p-95cb91] The rush of spring, Asad, torments the sight you find of the mind;
The cloud's a cotton plug that leaves the garden wall stone-blind to the mind.
