## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-54689d] The nations among which it is no longer admitted that one man can be the property of another are today numerous and powerful; and it is permissible to hope that in the future their influence will be stronger than that of peoples among whom contrary principles and practices still reign. However, when one compares the peoples among whom slavery is abolished to the peoples among whom the population is divided into slaves and masters; when one compares above all the extent of territory occupied by the former to the extent of territory occupied by the latter, one finds that slave-owners exercise, and will for a long time be able to exercise, an immense influence on the fate of the human race.

## Closes
> [p-f0a6e9] The laws of justice and morality do not bend according to our interests or our caprices; one must admit them for all men and for all nations, or renounce them for oneself. The moment that justice and morality cease to be universal, there is no longer either morality or justice for men; there is only a brutal force that one can sometimes be made to suffer, but which can also turn in an instant against those who have made it the rule of their judgments and their conduct.
