Anumāna-vyākhyāna complete, upamiti follows:

Someone asks "kim gavaya-h?" Hearing "gavaya-h go-sadṛśa-h" from one who knows, later seeing in vana an animal resembling go, thinking "ayam go-sadṛśo gavaya iti vyapadiśyate" determines "eso'yam gavaya-h" - this niścaya constitutes upamiti.

Here the kāraṇa is go-saṃsthāna-viśiṣṭa-artha regarding smaraṇa-vyāpāra. Some explain: pratyakṣa-darśana serves as upamāna while the resulting knowledge represents the pramiti-phala.

Others posit śabda-śakti operating through specific pravṛtti: just as "gām ānaya" generates go-directed action through śakti-graha and vyavahāra-understanding, so "gavayo go-sadṛśa-h" establishes word-meaning connections through anvaya-vyatireka patterns...
