...through their atomic nature. Space is singular and all-pervading, serving as the substrate for sound. Time and direction are also singular entities. Self and mind, though multiple in individual beings, are atomic in the supreme being [Ishvara]. Thus substances are established as ninefold.

Qualities are seventeen types, distinguished by color—white, blue, yellow, red, green, brown, and variegated. Taste [rasa] has six varieties: sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, and astringent. Smell [gandha] is of two types: pleasant and unpleasant, residing in earth. Touch [sparsha] is threefold: cold, hot, and neither, found in earth, water, fire, and air. These qualities are eternal in eternal atoms but non-eternal in composite substances like water pots. Number [sankhya] enables counting—one, two, etc.—being eternal in eternal substances, non-eternal in others. Magnitude [parimana] enables size distinctions—large, small, etc. Separateness [prithaktva] enables individual recognition. Conjunction [samyoga] brings about union, disjunction [vibhaga] brings separation. These, along with other actions, produce further conjunctions and disjunctions...
