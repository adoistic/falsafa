2

...as when hand-book conjunction produces hand-book conjunction, or pot-ground disjunction produces pot-space disjunction. Precedence and subsequence [paratva-aparatva] are temporal relations, while here-there [paratva-aparatva] are spatial relations. Priority and posteriority follow this pattern. Velocity [vega], understood as non-imperceptible motion, along with efforts leading to action and cessation—these constitute the fluidity quality [dravatva] that produces the liquid state in substances like oil. Sound [shabda], perceived by the ear, includes musical notes and syllables. Conjunction-born, disjunction-born qualities manifest through various combinations. Impressions [samskara] are of three types: velocity-born memory traces, and other forms. This completes the quality category.

Actions [karma] are five types: upward motion, downward motion, contraction, expansion, and general movement. These occur in substances excluding space, time, direction, self, and mind.

Universals [samanya] are of two types: higher universals like existence [satta], and lower universals like pot-ness [ghatatva]. Particulars [vishesha] are infinite, residing in eternal substances, distinguishing them ultimately. Inherence [samavaya] is the eternal relationship between whole and part, quality and substance, universal and particular, action and agent. These constitute the six categories of existence [bhava].
