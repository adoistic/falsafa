Usage-derived śakti-graha operates thus: through vyavahāra-darśana one understands that "gām ānaya" refers to that specific animal. This constitutes śabda-artha-saṃbandha knowledge unavailable through other pramāṇas.

Some reduce this to śabda-mātra, but incorrectly - upamāna generates distinctive knowledge of sadṛśya-saṃbandha through the synthesis of remembered śabda and present pratyakṣa-sādṛśya.

For śabda-pramāṇa generally: vākya-artha-bodha requires yogyatā-āsatti-tātparya coordination. Past temporal markers indicate pūrva-kālīna action, present markers show vartamāna-kriyā, future markers establish bhāviṣyat-vyāpāra.

Yogyatā means artha-saṃgati - "water burns" violates natural relationships and produces no valid understanding...
