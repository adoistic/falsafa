Sandigdha occurs when hetu appears in both sādhya-anvaya and sādhya-vyatireka locations, as "śabdo nitya-h prameya-tvāt" - since prameyatva characterizes both nitya and anitya dravyas.

Viruddha establishes sādhya-viparīta, as "śabdo nitya-h utpattimat-tvāt" - since utpatti actually demonstrates anityatva rather than nityatva.

Satpratipakṣa involves sama-bala counter-reasoning that neutralizes the original hetu through equally valid pratipakṣa demonstration.

Sādhyābhāvavat shows hetu present where sādhya is definitively absent, creating clear vyatireka that contradicts the intended sādhana.

Āśrayāsiddha questions the very existence of the pakṣa itself, as when the locus of inference remains unestablished.

Svarūpāsiddha challenges the hetu's own svarūpa, when the reason's existence or nature remains doubtful through pramāṇa-abhāva or other fundamental uncertainties...
