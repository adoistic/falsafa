...of samānādhikaraṇya. The āyudhana-saṃyoga: that selection among kevala-anvaya, kevala-vyatireka, and anvaya-vyatireka.

Kevala-anvaya is that which establishes through pure positive concomitance alone, as in "dravatva-ādi" and similar constructions. Kevala-vyatireka establishes through pure negative concomitance, as "that which differs from these bears guṇavattva-ādi" and similar demonstrations. Anvaya-vyatireka shows both movements, as "parvato vahnimān dhūmavat yathā mahānasa-ādayo na yathā hradādi-"

Anumāna divides into svārtha and parārtha. In the first, seeing dhūma on parvata, through vyāpti-smaraṇa comes the consciousness "yatra dhūmo vahnir api tatra" and thence "tasmād ayam parvato vahnimān" - this pratīti constitutes anumāna.

In parārtha: stating "ayam parvato vahnimān dhūmavat yathā mahānasa-ādih" involves pratijñā-hetu-udāharaṇa-upanaya-nigamana. This five-membered structure generates anumiti through specific cognitive operations involving vyāpti-paramarśa and related mental movements...
