Through contact between senses and their objects, perception [pratyaksha] is generated. Similarly, through contact with color etc., knowledge of substances conjoined or inherent in them arises; through contact with actions, knowledge of substances performing them through conjunction or inherence; through contact with universals existing in particulars through inherence, knowledge of substrates through inherence in inherents; through contact with conjunctions existing through inherence in conjuncts, knowledge through conjunction with inherents in inherence. When mind contacts these qualified objects, definite knowledge arises.

This constitutes ordinary perception [laukika pratyaksha]. When extraordinary perception [alaukika pratyaksha] occurs through universal-connection [samanyalakshana], it cognizes all past, present, and future objects possessing that universal. This completes the dual forms of perception.

Now inference [anumana]: This occurs when, knowing pervasion [vyapti], one cognizes "this mountain has fire" through seeing smoke. Inference means knowledge of the unperceived [aprameya] through the perceived [prameya]. When one knows "wherever there is smoke, there is fire" and then sees smoke on a mountain, one infers "this mountain has fire." This knowledge arises through inferential reasoning [anumana].
