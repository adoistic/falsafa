### Paragraph 1

This is the royal command of the Lord, the divine Buddha, this great instruction must be heeded, listen to all the `mantra`s. Lord Buddha was in the great dissolution (`mahāpralaya`), his body came into being, he then became the Lord Pañcatathāgata. Their bodies and names are distinct, each has a counterpart in form. His `mudrā` is the `bodhyagrī`, his `jñāna` is `śāśvata`, his implement is the `cakra`. Sahāvatī is the name of his dominion, a lion is his mount. She, Lady Sattvavajrī, she, Lady Dharmavajrī, she, Lady Karmavajrī, they are the `devī`s accompanying him. Now, as for his residence in the body, the inside of the heart is his abode.

### Paragraph 2

He, Lord Akṣobhya, resides in the East. Black is his color. She, Lady Locanā, is his `devī`. Hūṃ is his `mantra`. His `mudrā` is the `bhūsparśa`. His `jñāna` is `ādarśana`. The `vajra` is his implement. Abhirati is the name of his heaven. An elephant is his mount. Lord Vajrarāja, Lord Vajrarāga, the divine Vajrasādhu, Lord Vajradharādi, are those who accompany him. Now, as for his residence in the body, the inside of the lungs is his abode.

### Paragraph 3

He, Lord Ratnasambhava, resides in the South. Yellow is his color. She, Lady Māmakī, is his `devī`. Trāṃ is his `mantra`. His `jñāna` is `ākāśamata`. The `kapāla` is his implement. The inside of the spleen is his abode.

### Paragraph 4

He, Lord Amitābha, resides in the West. Red is his color. She, Lady Pāṇḍaravāsinī, is his `devī`. Hrīḥ is his `mantra`. His `jñāna` is `pratyavekṣaṇa`. His `mudrā` is the `dhyāna`. The `nāgapāśa` is his implement. Sukhāvatī is his heaven. He, Lord Vajradharma, Lord Vajratīkṣṇa, Lord Vajraketu, Lord Vajrabhāsa, are those who accompany him. Now, as for his residence in the body, the inside of the liver is his abode.

### Paragraph 5

He, Lord Amoghasiddhi, resides in the North. Variegated is his color. She, Lady Tārā, is his `devī`. Aḥ is his `mantra`. His `mudrā` is the `abhaya`. His `jñāna` is `kṛtyānuṣṭhāna`. Kusumita is the name of his heaven. A `garuḍa` is his mount. He, Lord Vajrakarma, Lord Vajrarakṣa, [Lord Vajrayakṣa,] Lord Vajrasandhi are those who accompany him. Now, as for his residence in the body, the inside of the gall bladder is his abode.

### Paragraph 5

Now then, as for the Lord Pañcatathāgata, having returned to their `lokadhātu`s, they are now in your body. They become the five wisdoms (`pañcajñāna`). The five wisdoms are called: `cakṣus`, `śrotra`, `ghrāṇa`, `jihvā`, `hṛdaya`. `cakṣus`, that is, the eyes; `śrotra`, that is, the ears; `ghrāṇa`, that is, the nose; `jihvā`, that is, the mouth; `hṛdaya`, that is, the liver. These are called the five senses (`pañcendriya`). Now, as for the deities of the five senses: Lord Akṣobhya resides in the eyes, Lord Ratnasambhava resides in the ears, Lord Amitābha resides in the nose, Lord Amoghasiddhi resides in the mouth. He who is the sense of taste is Lord Vairocana, residing in the heart, he is the mind. There also are their respective `devī`s. That is what is called the Divine Five-Souls (`Pañcātma`), which form the self.
