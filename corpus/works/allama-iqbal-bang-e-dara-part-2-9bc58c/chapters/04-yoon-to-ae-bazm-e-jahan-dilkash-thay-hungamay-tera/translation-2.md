O worldly stage, your spectacles were grand,
But sorrow in your onlookers was to be found.

The dust that roamed through Reason's desert-lands,
A peace for all such wanderers was to be found.

O Wine, how you have loved your hidden state;
Once freed from grapes, in crystal jars was to be found.

True Knowledge could not conquer Beauty's might;
Such folly in the world's wise ponderers was to be found.

I sought in vain through Europe's lands, Iqbal,
The grace that in Hindustan's heirs was to be found.
