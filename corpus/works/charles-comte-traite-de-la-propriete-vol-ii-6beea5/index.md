# Traité de la propriété: VOL II

Traité de la propriété (1834) by Charles Comte presents a sweeping philosophical and legal theory of private property. Comte argues that liberty is the foundation of rights, and that occupation and appropriation of things flow naturally from human freedom. He criticizes ancient jurisprudence grounded in slavery, claiming it undermines genuine property rights. He traces the formation of property from individual acquisition of things, through communal and national levels, exploring territory, value and legal guarantees. Comte thus frames property as essential for human development and social progress.
