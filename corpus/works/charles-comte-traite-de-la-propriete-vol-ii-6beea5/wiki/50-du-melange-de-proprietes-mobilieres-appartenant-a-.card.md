## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-b78e3a] Why is the right of accession subordinate to the principles of natural equity when it has for its object movable things, rather than when it has for its object immovable things? Would these principles, good for resolving the questions to which certain properties can give rise, be bad when it is a matter of resolving the questions that properties of another kind give rise to? Would the proprietors of lands be above the principles of natural equity, and must the rules of justice be applicable only to the proprietors of movable objects?
