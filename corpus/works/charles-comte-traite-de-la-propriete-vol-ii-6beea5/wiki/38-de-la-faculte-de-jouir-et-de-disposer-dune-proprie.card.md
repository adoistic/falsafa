## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-41f03d] If now we wish to indicate in a general manner the limits placed, by the very nature of things, on the enjoyment and disposition of all individual property, it will suffice for us to say that the proprietor can do with it all that he judges suitable, provided that he does not use it to infringe upon the security of persons, or upon the faculty that belongs to each, either to enjoy and dispose of his particular goods, or to make use, within the measure of his rights, of the goods that belong to his commune, his department, his nation, or to all of humanity.
