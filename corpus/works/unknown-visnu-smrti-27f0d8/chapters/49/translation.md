**Vishnu Smriti 49**

Having fasted on the eleventh day of the bright fortnight of the month of Mārgaśīrṣa, on the twelfth day he shall worship the blessed Vāsudeva. // Vi_49.1 //

With flowers, incense, ointments, lamps, and food offerings, and with propitiations of the fire and of Brāhmaṇas. // Vi_49.2 //

Having performed this vow for a year, he becomes pure from his sins. // Vi_49.3 //

Having performed it for his whole life, he attains the White Island. // Vi_49.4 //

By doing this on the twelfth days of both fortnights for a year, he attains the world of heaven. // Vi_49.5 //

For his whole life, the world of Vishnu. // Vi_49.6 //

And likewise on the fifteenth days also. // Vi_49.7 //

> Worshipping Keśava, who has become Brahman on the new moon day, and likewise on the full moon day, /
> And who has become Yoga, one attains greatness. || Vi_49.8 ||
>
> That full moon day on which the moon and Jupiter are seen together in the sky, /
> Is called the great one; it occurs once in a year. || Vi_49.9 ||
>
> On that day, charity, fasting, and the like are declared to be imperishable; /
> Likewise, the bright twelfth day which is conjoined with the Śravaṇa constellation. || Vi_49.10 ||
