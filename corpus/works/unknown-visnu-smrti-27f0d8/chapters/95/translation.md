**Vishnu Smriti 95**

A *vānaprastha* (वानप्रस्थ) shall emaciate his body with austerity (*tapas* (तपस्)). // Vi_95.1 //
In summer, he shall be one who endures five fires. // Vi_95.2 //
In the rainy season, one who sleeps under the open sky. // Vi_95.3 //
In winter, one who wears wet clothes. // Vi_95.4 //
He shall eat only at night. // Vi_95.5 //
Or he may eat every second, third, or fourth day. // Vi_95.6 //
He may eat flowers. // Vi_95.7 //
He may eat fruits. // Vi_95.8 //
He may eat vegetables. // Vi_95.9 //
Or he may eat leaves. // Vi_95.10 //
Or he may eat barley-gruel once at the end of a fortnight. // Vi_95.11 //
Or he may subsist on lunar penances. // Vi_95.12 //
He may pound his food with a stone. // Vi_95.13 //
Or use his teeth as a mortar. // Vi_95.14 //

> This entire world of gods and men has austerity (*tapas* (तपस्)) as its root, /
> Austerity as its middle, and austerity as its end; and by austerity it is thus supported. || Vi_95.15 ||
>
> What is hard to practice, what is hard to obtain, what is distant, and what is difficult to do, /
> All that is achievable by austerity (*tapas* (तपस्)); for austerity is indeed insurmountable. || Vi_95.16 ||
