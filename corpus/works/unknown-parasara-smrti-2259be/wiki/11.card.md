## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-adf2aa] Cow's urine, cow dung, milk, curd, clarified butter, and water with Kuśa grass—this is specified as *pañcagavya* (पञ्चगव्य), holy and purifying from sin. (II,1, p. 433) // Par_11.28 //

## Opens
> [p-b50cf8] Impure semen, cow's flesh, or the food of an outcaste (*caṇḍāla* चण्डाल)—if this is eaten by a Brāhmaṇa, he should perform the *Kṛcchra* (कृच्छ्र) and *Cāndrāyaṇa* (चान्द्रायण) penances. (II,1, p. 364) // Par_11.1 //

## Closes
> [p-db216a] the Gāyatrī, repeated ten thousand times, is the supreme purification. // Par_11.56 //
