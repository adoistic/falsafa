## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-520df4] Men who write on the science of legislation may, in general, be divided into two great classes. Some, strangers to the practice and even to the study of the laws of any country, engage only in philosophical considerations. Others, on the contrary, confine themselves strictly to the practice of law, and do not rise to any general views. The result is that those who wish to study the law often find, in the writings of the former, nothing but speculations of no real utility; and in the writings of the latter, only dissertations fit to interest litigants or their lawyers.
