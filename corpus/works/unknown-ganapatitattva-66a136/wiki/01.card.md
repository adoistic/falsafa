## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-448881] The syllable *ukāra* is first, followed by *a*, followed by *ma*. This is the dissolution of The Three-Syllable Mantra: *uṃ, aṃ, maṃ*. It is the cause of heaven. As for *u* and *akāra*, they dissolve into the *bindu* and crescent moon. The Lord dissolves into the *nāda*. The *nāda* has the void as its place. Such is the sequence, up to the condensed form of the fourteen syllables.
