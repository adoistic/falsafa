The Human Being

The scenes of the garden's expanse, whether beautiful or unadorned—
The Narcissus, deprived of action, is a compelled spectator.

It feels nothing of the pleasure of movement;
The Pine's very nature is devoid of longing.

Whatever exists in the world is accustomed to submission;
Every power of the Human Being is aflame with striving.

This single particle forever holds a yearning for vastness;
This is not a particle—perhaps it is a desert contracted to a point.

If it so chooses, it can change the very form of the garden;
This being is wise, is perceptive, is potent.
