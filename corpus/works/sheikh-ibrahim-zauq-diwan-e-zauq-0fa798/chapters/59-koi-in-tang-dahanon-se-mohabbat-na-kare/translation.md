Let no one love those lips of cruel design, and offer no complaint;
And when they bring distress, by word or sign, let them offer no complaint.

My heart sees in love's scar a prophet's seal of grace;
Let not this infidel then claim a prophet's place, and offer no complaint.

For my wound, the only cure is dust of diamond bright;
Let healing salves and stones bring it no light, and offer no complaint.

A river from my tears flows with my every stride;
Let not the path keep pace, or travel by my side, and offer no complaint.

The dagger's tongue is wet with my blood to this day;
Let no one ask for witness to this deadly play, and offer no complaint.

He is the favored heir of some great master's art;
Let no one else presume to play his sovereign part, and offer no complaint.

The moth can't burn until the candle shows its flame;
Let beauty lead the dance and first call out love's name, and offer no complaint.

The killer walks again to where his lovers lie;
Let him not raise a final doom upon the slain, and offer no complaint.
