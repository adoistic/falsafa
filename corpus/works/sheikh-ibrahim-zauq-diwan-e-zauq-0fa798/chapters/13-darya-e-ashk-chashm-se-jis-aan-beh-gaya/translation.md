When from my eyes the river of tears held sway,
The very throne-room of the heavens was swept away.

Love's crucible made my heart its bleeding prey,
The arrowhead of your glance was swept away.

O, zealot, why for drinking wine must I pray?
In just a sip, was all my faith swept away?

Love's ocean is a storm all things obey,
A helpless fist of dust was swept away.

I wrote my heart's lament in tears today,
And like a boat, my pen-case was swept away.

The water-blisters burst in weeping, gray,
A lonely wail to wastelands was swept away.

O, ruby of Badakhshan, on proud display,
Before her lips, your value was swept away.

My life's a vessel, Zauq, on death's dark bay;
When the great storm arrived, I was swept away.

Once Delhi's glory, Zauq, knew no decay,
But now its vital spirit is swept away.
