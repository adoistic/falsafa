## Key passage (TextRank #1)
> [p-3114c0] To acknowledge oneself a slave is not only to abdicate one's rights, it is moreover to renounce the accomplishment of one's duties; it is to recognize that one is bound to nothing, neither toward oneself, nor toward others; it is to proclaim a contradiction: for if, by his nature, man is bound to nothing, neither toward himself, nor toward others, how could he be bound to anything toward a master?
